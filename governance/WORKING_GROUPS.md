# GeoDistricts DAO Working Groups

This document outlines the structure, formation, and operation of working groups (WGs) in the GeoDistricts DAO, which handle specialized domains and prepare proposals for token holder approval.

## Working Group Framework

### Purpose and Structure
Working groups are specialized committees that focus on specific domains of the GeoDistricts ecosystem. They prepare proposals, conduct research, and execute approved initiatives under token holder oversight.

### WG Categories

#### Core Protocol WGs (Permanent)
- **Protocol WG**: GDIP development, protocol improvements, technical standards
- **Development WG**: Reference implementation, tooling, infrastructure
- **Treasury WG**: Financial management, yield strategies, budget proposals
- **Community WG**: Education, outreach, ecosystem partnerships

#### Specialized WGs (Temporary/Projects)
- **Research WG**: Academic partnerships, data analysis, legal research
- **Marketing WG**: Brand development, content creation, media relations
- **Security WG**: Smart contract audits, bug bounties, security monitoring
- **Integration WG**: Third-party tools, API development, interoperability

## Working Group Formation

### Formation Process

#### 1. Proposal Submission
```markdown
# Working Group Formation Proposal

## WG Overview
- **Name**: [WG Name]
- **Category**: [Core/Specialized]
- **Duration**: [Permanent/Temporary with end date]
- **Budget Request**: [Quarterly budget allocation]

## Mission Statement
[Clear description of WG purpose and scope]

## Initial Members
[List of proposed founding members with relevant expertise]

## Success Metrics
[Measurable goals and KPIs for the WG]

## Operations Plan
[Meeting schedule, decision-making process, reporting requirements]
```

#### 2. Governance Approval
- **Forum Discussion**: 14 days for community feedback
- **Snapshot Vote**: Simple majority approval required
- **Budget Allocation**: Approved quarterly budget via treasury proposal

#### 3. Launch and Onboarding
- **Member Onboarding**: Training on WG operations and DAO processes
- **Tool Setup**: Dedicated communication channels and collaboration tools
- **Initial Planning**: First 30 days for roadmap development and goal setting

## Working Group Operations

### Membership Structure

#### Core Members (3-7 members)
- **Selection**: Elected by token holders or appointed by existing WGs
- **Term Length**: 6 months with community recall option
- **Responsibilities**: Active participation, proposal preparation, execution
- **Compensation**: Retroactive funding or grant-based rewards

#### Contributors (Open participation)
- **Eligibility**: Any community member with relevant expertise
- **Participation**: Voluntary or compensated based on contributions
- **Rights**: Proposal input, meeting attendance, working document access
- **Path to Core**: Proven contributors can become core members

#### Advisors (Domain experts)
- **Role**: Provide specialized knowledge and strategic guidance
- **Selection**: Invited by core members or community nomination
- **Compensation**: Honorarium or retroactive funding
- **Term**: Rolling basis, renewed annually

### Decision-Making Process

#### Internal WG Governance
- **Consensus-Driven**: Decisions made through discussion and agreement
- **Voting Threshold**: Simple majority for routine decisions
- **Escalation**: Unresolved issues escalated to full DAO vote
- **Transparency**: All discussions and decisions publicly documented

#### Proposal Development
1. **Research Phase**: WG conducts analysis and gathers data
2. **Draft Preparation**: Working document shared with community
3. **Community Review**: 7-14 days for feedback and refinement
4. **Final Proposal**: Submitted to Snapshot for token holder approval

### Meeting and Communication

#### Regular Cadence
- **Weekly Meetings**: Core member coordination and progress updates
- **Bi-weekly Community Calls**: Open sessions for contributor input
- **Monthly Reports**: Progress updates and upcoming initiatives
- **Quarterly Reviews**: Comprehensive assessment and planning

#### Communication Channels
- **Discord Category**: Dedicated text/voice channels for WG discussions
- **Forum Section**: Long-form discussions and proposal drafts
- **GitHub Repository**: Documentation, meeting notes, and deliverables
- **Snapshot Discussions**: Community feedback on proposals

## Working Group Responsibilities

### Protocol WG
- **GDIP Development**: Create and maintain protocol improvement proposals
- **Technical Standards**: Define implementation standards and best practices
- **Protocol Audits**: Coordinate security reviews and testing
- **Version Management**: Oversee protocol releases and compatibility

### Development WG
- **Reference Implementation**: Maintain and improve geodistricts.org
- **Tool Development**: Create developer tools, SDKs, and integrations
- **Performance Optimization**: Monitor and improve system performance
- **Infrastructure**: Manage deployment, scaling, and reliability

### Treasury WG
- **Yield Strategies**: Research and implement treasury yield opportunities
- **Budget Proposals**: Prepare quarterly spending plans and allocations
- **Financial Reporting**: Monthly treasury reports and quarterly audits
- **Risk Management**: Monitor treasury security and diversification

### Community WG
- **Education Programs**: Develop tutorials, documentation, and training
- **Outreach Initiatives**: Community events, partnerships, and media
- **Ecosystem Grants**: Administer contributor compensation programs
- **Growth Metrics**: Track community engagement and adoption

## Performance and Accountability

### Success Metrics
- **Proposal Success Rate**: Percentage of WG proposals approved by token holders
- **Budget Utilization**: Efficient use of allocated resources
- **Community Impact**: Measurable benefits delivered to the ecosystem
- **Member Satisfaction**: Internal surveys and retention rates

### Evaluation Process
- **Quarterly Reviews**: WG performance assessment by token holders
- **Member Recall**: Community can remove underperforming members
- **Budget Adjustments**: Funding modified based on demonstrated value
- **WG Dissolution**: Underperforming WGs can be disbanded

### Recognition and Rewards
- **Retroactive Funding**: Monthly rewards for demonstrated impact
- **Grant Opportunities**: Additional funding for successful initiatives
- **Public Acknowledgment**: Featured in monthly ecosystem reports
- **Career Advancement**: Successful WG members gain reputation and opportunities

## Working Group Lifecycle

### Formation and Growth
1. **Proposal and Approval**: Initial formation via governance vote
2. **Member Recruitment**: Build core team and contributor base
3. **Capability Development**: Training and resource acquisition
4. **Operational Maturity**: Full workflow and delivery capability

### Evolution and Adaptation
- **Scope Expansion**: WGs can propose expanded responsibilities
- **Sub-group Creation**: Form specialized sub-committees for complex tasks
- **Cross-WG Collaboration**: Joint initiatives between related working groups
- **Process Optimization**: Continuous improvement of WG operations

### Dissolution and Transition
- **Completion Criteria**: Temporary WGs dissolve upon project completion
- **Performance Triggers**: Low-performing WGs may be restructured
- **Knowledge Transfer**: Documentation and handover to successor groups
- **Asset Distribution**: Budget and resources reallocated to active initiatives

## Integration with Broader Governance

### Relationship to Delegates
- **Specialization**: WGs focus on execution, delegates on strategic oversight
- **Collaboration**: WGs prepare proposals that delegates may champion
- **Feedback Loop**: Delegates provide strategic guidance to WGs
- **Complementary Roles**: WGs handle operations, delegates handle governance

### Escalation Pathways
- **Internal Resolution**: WG attempts to resolve issues internally
- **Delegate Mediation**: Domain delegates assist in conflict resolution
- **Governance Vote**: Unresolved issues escalated to full DAO
- **Emergency Council**: Critical issues activate emergency procedures

This working group framework enables specialized, efficient execution while maintaining community oversight and accountability.
