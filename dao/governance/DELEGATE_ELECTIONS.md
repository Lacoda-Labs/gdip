# GeoDistricts DAO Delegate Election Process

This document outlines the election, term management, and responsibilities of delegates in the GeoDistricts DAO governance system.

## Delegate System Overview

### Purpose
Delegates serve as elected representatives who provide specialized oversight and strategic guidance for specific domains of the GeoDistricts ecosystem. They act as a bridge between working groups and full token holder governance.

### Delegate Categories

#### Domain Delegates (Permanent Positions)
- **Technical Delegate**: Protocol development, reference implementation, security
- **Legal Delegate**: Regulatory compliance, legal strategy, intellectual property
- **Community Delegate**: Education, outreach, ecosystem partnerships, diversity
- **Treasury Delegate**: Financial management, yield strategies, budget oversight

#### Specialized Delegates (Temporary/Project-based)
- **Research Delegate**: Academic partnerships, data analysis initiatives
- **Marketing Delegate**: Brand development, content strategy, media relations
- **Integration Delegate**: Third-party tools, API development, interoperability
- **Emergency Delegate**: Activated during crisis situations

## Election Process

### Nomination Phase (Days 1-14)

#### Self-Nomination
- **Eligibility**: Any token holder with minimum balance (100 $GEOD)
- **Application Requirements**:
  - Personal background and relevant expertise
  - Vision statement for the delegate role
  - Commitment to time investment (10+ hours/week)
  - Conflict of interest disclosures

### Campaign Phase (Days 15-21)

#### Community Engagement
- **Forum Discussions**: Candidates participate in Q&A sessions
- **Town Halls**: Live sessions for detailed policy discussions
- **Position Papers**: Detailed proposals on key issues
- **Endorsements**: Community members can publicly support candidates

#### Information Distribution
- **Candidate Directory**: Centralized list of all nominees with profiles
- **Comparison Tools**: Side-by-side comparison of candidate positions
- **Voting Guides**: Educational content on delegate roles and responsibilities

- **Multi-seat Elections**: Voters can support multiple candidates per domain
- **Ranked Choice**: Voters rank preferred candidates for each delegate position
- **Delegation Option**: Token holders can delegate voting power to trusted voters

#### Voting Process
1. **Registration**: Voters prove token ownership via snapshot
2. **Ballot Creation**: Select and rank candidates for each delegate position
3. **Vote Submission**: Secure submission with quadratic weighting
4. **Results Calculation**: Ranked choice algorithm determines winners

#### Election Parameters
- **Quorum Requirement**: 5% of circulating supply must participate
- **Winning Threshold**: Top candidates by ranked choice scoring
- **Tie Resolution**: Random selection for tied candidates
- **Run-off Elections**: If no clear winner emerges

### Inauguration Phase (Days 29-30)

#### Transition Period
- **Winner Announcement**: Public declaration of elected delegates
- **Handover Process**: Outgoing delegates brief incoming delegates
- **Onboarding Program**: Training on delegate responsibilities and tools
- **Initial Planning**: First 30 days for delegate team formation

## Delegate Responsibilities

### Core Duties

#### Strategic Oversight
- **Working Group Guidance**: Provide strategic direction to relevant WGs
- **Proposal Review**: Evaluate WG proposals before community voting
- **Cross-domain Coordination**: Ensure alignment between different ecosystem areas
- **Long-term Planning**: Develop multi-quarter roadmaps and objectives

#### Community Representation
- **Stakeholder Engagement**: Regular communication with ecosystem participants
- **Feedback Collection**: Gather community input on governance and operations
- **Dispute Mediation**: Help resolve conflicts between WGs or community members
- **Transparency Reporting**: Regular updates on delegate activities and decisions

#### Governance Facilitation
- **Proposal Sponsorship**: Champion important proposals through the process
- **Process Improvement**: Identify and implement governance enhancements
- **Emergency Response**: Activate emergency procedures when needed
- **Succession Planning**: Prepare for delegate transitions and knowledge transfer

### Performance Expectations

#### Activity Metrics
- **Meeting Attendance**: 80%+ attendance at WG and community meetings
- **Proposal Engagement**: Review and provide feedback on 90% of relevant proposals
- **Communication Response**: 24-hour response time for urgent matters
- **Reporting Frequency**: Weekly updates and monthly comprehensive reports

#### Impact Metrics
- **WG Productivity**: Measurable improvements in WG output quality
- **Community Satisfaction**: Positive feedback from ecosystem participants
- **Proposal Success**: Higher approval rates for delegate-sponsored proposals
- **Conflict Resolution**: Successful mediation of ecosystem disputes

## Term Management

### Term Structure
- **Length**: 6 months from election date
- **Renewal**: Delegates can run for re-election
- **Staggering**: Elections staggered to maintain continuity
- **Recall Process**: Community can remove delegates mid-term

### Recall Mechanism

#### Recall Triggers
- **Performance Issues**: Failure to meet activity or impact metrics
- **Ethical Violations**: Breach of community standards or conflicts of interest
- **Community Petition**: 10% of token holders can initiate recall vote
- **Self-Removal**: Delegates can resign voluntarily

#### Recall Process
1. **Petition Submission**: Formal petition with evidence of issues
2. **Investigation Period**: 7 days for fact-finding and delegate response
3. **Community Vote**: 3-day recall election with quadratic voting
4. **Succession**: Immediate replacement if recall succeeds

### Succession Planning

#### Knowledge Transfer
- **Documentation**: Comprehensive handover notes for each domain
- **Mentorship**: Outgoing delegates mentor incoming delegates
- **Institutional Memory**: Maintain records of decisions and rationales
- **Process Continuity**: Ensure smooth transition of ongoing initiatives

#### Interim Governance
- **Acting Delegates**: Temporary appointments during transition periods
- **Emergency Council**: Multi-sig activation for critical decisions
- **WG Autonomy**: Working groups continue operations during transitions
- **Community Oversight**: Enhanced monitoring during leadership changes

## Delegate Compensation

### Reward Structure
- **Base Compensation**: Monthly $GEOD stipend ($5,000-10,000 based on domain complexity)
- **Performance Bonuses**: Additional rewards for exceeding impact metrics
- **Retroactive Funding**: Monthly rounds reward demonstrated contributions
- **Expense Reimbursement**: Approved travel and operational costs

### Incentive Alignment
- **Long-term Holding**: Portion of compensation in vested $GEOD
- **Impact-based Rewards**: Bonuses tied to measurable ecosystem improvements
- **Community Recognition**: Public acknowledgment and reputation building
- **Career Opportunities**: Successful delegates gain ecosystem influence
